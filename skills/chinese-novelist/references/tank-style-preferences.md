# Tank-Style Urban Fiction Preferences（超级大坦克科比风格偏好）

> User-specific preferences for writing Chinese urban fiction in the style of 超级大坦克科比 (Super Tank Kobe).

## Style Profile

- **Genre**: 都市现实主义 (Urban Realism)
- **Tone**: 虐心 + 黑色幽默 (Bittersweet + Dark Humor)
- **POV**: 第一人称男 (First Person Male) — classic Tank voice
- **Pace**: 慢 (Slow, detailed)
- **Chapter length**: ~6000字

## Character Constraints

- **Male lead**: MUST be truly ordinary. NOT wealthy. Working-class background. Delivery rider, factory worker, small shop owner — someone living paycheck to paycheck. Explicitly NOT like Tank's protagonists who are comfortable middle-class.
- **Female lead**: Not a "rich girl" trope. She climbed up through effort (self-study, job-hopping). The gap is one of upward mobility, not inherited wealth.
- **Age**: 23–25 years old. Young enough that the past is still raw, old enough that life has already taken divergent paths.
- **No cultivation / fantasy / system elements**. Purely realist.

## Setting

- **City**: 深圳 (Shenzhen) — the definitive migrant-worker city in China. Tech parks next to urban villages, delivery riders in the shadow of skyscrapers.
- **Key locations**: 白石洲 (Baishizhou, iconic urban village), 龙华 (Longhua, factory district), 科技园 (Tech Park, office zone).

## Core Theme: Reunion + Class Divide

The user's preferred story template:
- Male and female leads met as migrant workers (factory, restaurant, construction)
- They were together briefly — the best time of his life
- She wanted more, he was content
- They split. Years pass.
- **Reunion**: He's still at the bottom (delivery rider). She's climbed into an office job.
- The bitterness comes from life itself — no villain, just time and circumstance.

## Tank-Style Execution Rules

1. **Show daily life in granular detail** — what he eats, how much rent costs, the weight of the delivery bag, the sweat.
2. **Dialogue is real** — people interrupt, change topics, don't say what they mean.
3. **The reunion moment is understated** — no melodrama. A pause. An awkward greeting. The weight is in what's NOT said.
4. **Nostalgia is the engine** — flashbacks to the good days make the present gap hurt more.
5. **End each chapter with an emotional hook**, not a plot cliffhanger. A feeling, a detail, a silence.

## Prohibited

- 霸道总裁 / 爽文 tropes
- Overly poetic or flowery narration (Tank writes simply)
- Characters explaining their feelings explicitly
- Artificial drama (misunderstandings that could be solved with one sentence)
